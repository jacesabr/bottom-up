Some Applications of Trigonometry

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 19 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Solve a heights-and-distances problem with trigonometry — Given a real-world height/distance situation, model it as right triangle(s), pick the correct trig ratio, and solve for the unknown length.
  2. Draw and label the right triangle from a word problem — Translate the verbal scene into a right triangle, marking the right angle, the given angle, the known side, and the unknown side.
  3. Identify the angle of elevation — Recognise the angle of elevation as the angle the upward line of sight makes with the horizontal.
  4. Identify the angle of depression — Recognise the angle of depression as the angle the downward line of sight makes with the horizontal.
  5. Convert an angle of depression to an angle inside the triangle (alternate angles) — Use that the angle of depression equals the alternate interior angle at the lower point because the two horizontals are parallel.
  6. Choose tangent when opposite and adjacent sides are involved — Pick tan of the known angle when the unknown and known sides are the side opposite and the side adjacent to that angle.
  7. Choose sine when opposite side and hypotenuse are involved — Pick sin of the known angle when the relevant sides are the side opposite the angle and the hypotenuse.
  8. Recall the trig ratio value of a standard angle — Substitute the exact value of sin, cos, tan, or cot at 30°, 45°, or 60°.
  9. Set up the trig equation relating the unknown to known values — Write the equation 'ratio of known angle = (one side)/(other side)' with the known length substituted.
  10. Solve the trig equation for the unknown side — Rearrange the equation (multiply/divide) to isolate the unknown length.
  11. Compute a hypotenuse length using sine — From sin(angle) = opposite/hypotenuse with the opposite known, solve for the hypotenuse.
  12. Compute the horizontal base using cot — Use cot(angle) = adjacent/opposite to find the horizontal distance from the foot.
  13. Add the observer's height to the triangle result — When the angle is measured from an observer's eye, add the eye height to the computed leg to get the full object height.
  14. Model a problem with two right triangles sharing a side — When two angles are given for one base or one object, build two right triangles sharing a common side and write an equation from each.
  15. Solve two trig equations by substitution — Eliminate one unknown by substituting one trig equation into the other and solve for the remaining unknown.
  16. Rationalise a surd denominator — Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate to remove a surd from the denominator.
  17. Approximate a surd answer to a decimal — Substitute the given value of √3 (e.g. 1.732) to convert an exact surd answer to a decimal.
  18. Combine segment lengths to get the asked quantity — Add or subtract the computed pieces (sub-lengths) to produce the final asked height/width/distance.
  19. Use that a 45° right triangle has equal legs — Recognise that when the angle is 45°, the opposite and adjacent legs are equal (tan45 = 1).
