Areas Related to Circles

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 13 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Find the area of a segment of a circle — Compute the area of a segment as the area of the corresponding sector minus the area of the triangle formed by the two radii and the chord.
  2. Apply the sector-area formula θ/360 × πr² — Compute the area of a sector of angle θ and radius r using Area = θ/360 × πr².
  3. Derive sector area by the Unitary Method — Reason that a 360° angle gives area πr², so 1° gives πr²/360, hence θ° gives θ/360 × πr².
  4. Compute the length of an arc of a sector — Find arc length as θ/360 of the full circumference 2πr.
  5. Compute the area of the major sector — Find the major sector area either as πr² minus the minor sector, or with angle (360 − θ).
  6. Compute triangle area as ½ × base × height — Find the area of triangle OAB using ½ × AB × OM, where OM is the perpendicular height.
  7. Drop a perpendicular from centre to chord — Draw OM ⊥ AB; by RHS congruence M is the midpoint of AB and OM bisects the central angle.
  8. Use RHS congruence to halve the central angle — By RHS, ΔAMO ≅ ΔBMO, so ∠AOM = ∠BOM = ½ of the central angle.
  9. Use cos 60° to find the perpendicular OM — From right triangle OMA, OM/OA = cos 60° = ½, giving OM = r/2.
  10. Use sin 60° to find half-chord AM — From right triangle OMA, AM/OA = sin 60° = √3/2, giving AM = (r√3)/2.
  11. Find chord length AB = 2 × AM — Since the perpendicular bisects the chord, the full chord AB is twice the half-chord AM.
  12. Subtract triangle from sector to get the segment — Combine the computed sector area and triangle area: segment = sector − triangle.
  13. Substitute the given value of π and evaluate — Substitute π = 22/7 or π = 3.14 as instructed, then carry out the arithmetic.
