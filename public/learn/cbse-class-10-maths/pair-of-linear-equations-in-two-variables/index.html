Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 21 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Solve a pair of linear equations in two variables — Given a pair of linear equations (or a word problem reducible to one), determine whether a solution exists and find it by an appropriate method.
  2. Classify a pair using the ratios \(a_1/a_2\), \(b_1/b_2\), \(c_1/c_2\) — Compare the three coefficient ratios to decide whether the lines intersect (unique), coincide (infinite), or are parallel (none).
  3. Label a pair consistent, inconsistent, or dependent — Map the geometric situation to the vocabulary: solution exists = consistent, none = inconsistent, infinitely many equivalent = dependent.
  4. Find two solutions of a linear equation for plotting — Choose convenient x-values, compute y, to get two points that determine the line.
  5. Plot points and draw the line of an equation — Place ordered pairs on the coordinate plane and join them with a straight line.
  6. Read the intersection point as the solution — Identify the common point of the two drawn lines and report it as x = , y = .
  7. Solve a pair graphically — Draw both lines from tabulated points and obtain the solution (or conclude none/infinite) from their geometric relation.
  8. Express one variable in terms of the other — Rearrange one equation to isolate x (or y) as an expression in the other variable.
  9. Substitute the expression into the other equation — Replace the isolated variable in the second equation, producing one equation in one variable.
  10. Solve the resulting one-variable equation — Solve a linear equation in a single variable to get its value.
  11. Back-substitute to find the other variable — Plug the found value into the Step-1 expression/equation to get the remaining variable.
  12. Solve a pair by the substitution method — Express, substitute, solve, and back-substitute to find x and y (or detect no/infinite solutions).
  13. Interpret a true no-variable statement as infinitely many solutions — When substitution/elimination yields an identity like 18 = 18, conclude infinitely many solutions.
  14. Interpret a false no-variable statement as no solution — When the variable cancels leaving a false statement like −4 = 0, conclude the pair is inconsistent.
  15. Multiply equations to make a coefficient numerically equal — Scale each equation by a non-zero constant so one variable's coefficients match in magnitude.
  16. Add or subtract equations to eliminate a variable — Combine the scaled equations so one variable cancels, leaving one equation in one unknown.
  17. Solve a pair by the elimination method — Equalise a coefficient, eliminate that variable, solve, and back-substitute.
  18. Form a pair of linear equations from a word problem — Assign variables to unknowns and translate each stated condition into a linear equation.
  19. Model a two-digit number and its reversal by place value — Write a two-digit number as 10x + y and its reversal as 10y + x.
  20. Verify the solution satisfies both original equations — Substitute the found x and y back into the originals (or the problem's conditions) to confirm correctness.
  21. Find an unknown coefficient k giving a required solution type — Use the ratio conditions to solve for a parameter that makes a pair inconsistent/consistent/dependent.
