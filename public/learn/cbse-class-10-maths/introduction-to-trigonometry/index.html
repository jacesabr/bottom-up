Introduction to Trigonometry

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 11 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Define sin, cos, tan from a right triangle — State the three primary trigonometric ratios of an acute angle as ratios of the opposite side, adjacent side, and hypotenuse of a right triangle.
  2. Define cosec, sec, cot as reciprocals — Define the reciprocal trigonometric ratios cosec, sec, and cot as the reciprocals of sin, cos, and tan respectively.
  3. Express tan and cot via sin and cos — Use the quotient relations tan A = sin A/cos A and cot A = cos A/sin A to rewrite ratios in terms of sine and cosine.
  4. Find all ratios given one ratio — Given one trigonometric ratio of an acute angle, construct the triangle and determine all the remaining ratios.
  5. Trigonometric ratios of 45 degrees — Recall the exact trigonometric ratios of 45 degrees from an isosceles right triangle.
  6. Trigonometric ratios of 30 and 60 degrees — Recall the exact trigonometric ratios of 30 and 60 degrees from an equilateral triangle bisected by an altitude.
  7. Trigonometric ratios of 0 and 90 degrees — State the limiting values of the trigonometric ratios at 0 and 90 degrees, including which are undefined.
  8. Evaluate expressions at specific angles — Substitute the exact values of the specific angles into a numeric trigonometric expression and simplify.
  9. Apply the identity sin^2 A + cos^2 A = 1 — Derive and apply the fundamental identity sin^2 A + cos^2 A = 1 from the Pythagoras theorem.
  10. Apply the identity 1 + tan^2 A = sec^2 A — Derive and apply the identity 1 + tan^2 A = sec^2 A and its cot/cosec counterpart.
  11. Prove and simplify trigonometric identities — Prove a trigonometric identity by transforming one side using the reciprocal, quotient, and Pythagorean identities until it equals the other.
