Polynomials

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 21 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Relate zeroes and coefficients of a polynomial — The chapter's top skill: given a quadratic or cubic polynomial, find its zeroes and connect them to the coefficients via the sum/product relations (and the reverse — build a polynomial from its zeroes).
  2. Identify the degree of a polynomial — Read off the highest power of the variable as the degree of the polynomial.
  3. Classify a polynomial as linear, quadratic, or cubic — Name a polynomial by its degree: degree 1 linear, degree 2 quadratic, degree 3 cubic.
  4. Recognise expressions that are not polynomials — Reject expressions with the variable in a denominator or under a root as non-polynomials.
  5. Evaluate a polynomial p(x) at x = k — Substitute a number for the variable and compute the resulting value p(k).
  6. Evaluate a polynomial at a negative argument — Substitute a negative number, correctly squaring/cubing the sign.
  7. Define a zero of a polynomial — A real number k is a zero of p(x) precisely when p(k)=0.
  8. Find the zero of a linear polynomial — Set ax+b=0 and solve to get the single zero −b/a.
  9. Interpret a zero as an x-intercept of the graph — Read the zeroes of p(x) as the x-coordinates where y=p(x) crosses the x-axis.
  10. Count the number of zeroes from a graph — Count how many times the graph of y=p(x) meets the x-axis to get the number of zeroes.
  11. Bound the number of zeroes by the degree — A polynomial of degree n has at most n zeroes.
  12. Factorise a quadratic by splitting the middle term — Split the middle term into two terms whose product equals (coeff of x²)×(constant)×x², then group.
  13. Factorise using the difference-of-squares identity — Apply a²−b²=(a−b)(a+b) to factor a quadratic with no middle term.
  14. Find zeroes from factored form via zero-product — Set each linear factor equal to zero and solve to get the zeroes.
  15. Verify the sum of zeroes equals −b/a — Check that the sum of the two zeroes equals −(coefficient of x)/(coefficient of x²).
  16. Verify the product of zeroes equals c/a — Check that the product of the two zeroes equals (constant term)/(coefficient of x²).
  17. State the general sum/product relations for a quadratic — Recall α+β=−b/a and αβ=c/a for zeroes of ax²+bx+c.
  18. Build a quadratic from given sum and product of zeroes — Use α+β=−b/a, αβ=c/a with a=1 to write a quadratic with the given sum and product.
  19. Verify a value is a zero of a cubic by substitution — Substitute a candidate into the cubic and confirm the result is 0.
  20. State the sum/pair-sum/product relations for a cubic — Recall α+β+γ=−b/a, αβ+βγ+γα=c/a, αβγ=−d/a for ax³+bx²+cx+d.
  21. Verify the cubic zero–coefficient relations numerically — Compute the sum, pair-sum, and product of the three zeroes and match them to −b/a, c/a, −d/a.
