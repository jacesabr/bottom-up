Circles

This chapter of CBSE Class 10 Maths covers 20 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Solve tangent-to-a-circle problems — Apply tangent properties (perpendicularity, equal lengths) to compute lengths/angles and prove results in circle figures.
  2. Define a tangent to a circle — A tangent is a line meeting the circle at exactly one point (the point of contact).
  3. Classify a line relative to a circle — Decide whether a line is non-intersecting, a secant, or a tangent by its number of common points.
  4. Count tangents from a point by its location — 0 tangents from inside, 1 from on the circle, exactly 2 from an external point.
  5. Apply tangent perpendicular to radius — The radius drawn to the point of contact is perpendicular to the tangent there.
  6. Justify why a tangent point lies outside — Any point on the tangent other than the contact point lies outside the circle, so its distance from the centre exceeds the radius.
  7. Apply equal lengths of tangents from external point — Two tangents drawn from the same external point to a circle are equal in length.
  8. Prove equal tangents via RHS congruence — Join centre to point and to contact points; right triangles are congruent by RHS, so tangents are equal by CPCT.
  9. Compute tangent length using Pythagoras — In the right triangle radius-tangent-line-to-centre, tangent length = sqrt(distance^2 - radius^2).
  10. Find chord of larger circle touching smaller concentric circle — The chord of the larger circle tangent to the smaller is bisected at contact; use right triangle with the two radii.
  11. Use perpendicular from centre bisects the chord — A line from the centre perpendicular to a chord bisects that chord.
  12. Relate angle between tangents to central angle — Angle between two tangents from an external point plus the central angle of the contact chord equals 180 degrees.
  13. Prove angle PTQ equals twice angle OPQ — Using isosceles tangent triangle and the right angle at the contact point, derive PTQ = 2·OPQ.
  14. Compute base angles of isosceles tangent triangle — In an isosceles triangle each base angle is (180 - apex)/2.
  15. Use equal tangent segments in a circumscribed quadrilateral — For a quadrilateral circumscribing a circle, equal tangent segments give AB + CD = AD + BC.
  16. Find triangle sides from inscribed-circle tangent segments — Use equal tangent segments from each vertex of a triangle with an inscribed circle to find unknown sides.
  17. Use similar right triangles to find a tangent length — Set up AA-similar right triangles in a tangent-chord figure and use the proportion to find a length.
  18. Prove tangents at diameter ends are parallel — Both tangents are perpendicular to the same diameter, so they are parallel.
  19. Solve a right triangle with the Pythagoras theorem — Relate the three sides of a right triangle by hypotenuse² = leg² + leg².
  20. Apply RHS congruence and CPCT — Two right triangles with equal hypotenuse and one equal side are congruent (RHS); corresponding parts are then equal (CPCT).
