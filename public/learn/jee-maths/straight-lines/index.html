Straight Lines

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 16 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Identify the inclination of a line with the x-axis — Recognise the inclination as the anticlockwise angle a line makes with the positive x-axis, lying between 0 and 180 degrees.
  2. Define the slope of a line as tan of its inclination — Define slope m = tan(theta) from the inclination, and recognise when it is zero or undefined.
  3. Compute the slope from the coordinates of two points — Find the slope of a non-vertical line through two given points as the ratio of differences of their coordinates.
  4. Test two lines for parallelism using their slopes — Use the rule that two non-vertical lines are parallel if and only if their slopes are equal.
  5. Test two lines for perpendicularity using their slopes — Use the rule that two non-vertical lines are perpendicular if and only if the product of their slopes is -1.
  6. Find the acute angle between two lines from their slopes — Compute the acute angle between two intersecting non-vertical lines using the tangent formula in their slopes.
  7. Test three points for collinearity using equal slopes — Decide whether three points lie on one line by checking that the slope of one segment equals the slope of an adjacent segment.
  8. Write the equations of horizontal and vertical lines — Give the equation y = a or y = -a for a horizontal line and x = b or x = -b for a vertical line at a fixed distance from an axis.
  9. Write a line's equation in point-slope form — Derive and apply y - y0 = m(x - x0) for a line of slope m through a fixed point (x0, y0).
  10. Write a line's equation in two-point form — Derive and apply the equation of a line through two given points using the slope between them.
  11. Write a line's equation in slope-intercept form — Use y = mx + c for a line with slope m and y-intercept c, and the variant y = m(x - d) for a given x-intercept.
  12. Write a line's equation in intercept form — Use x/a + y/b = 1 for a line with x-intercept a and y-intercept b on the axes.
  13. Recognise the general equation of a line Ax + By + C = 0 — Recognise that every line is Ax + By + C = 0 with A, B not both zero, and convert between this and the other forms.
  14. Compute the perpendicular distance of a point from a line — Apply d = |A x1 + B y1 + C| / sqrt(A^2 + B^2) for the distance of a point from a line in general form.
  15. Compute the distance between two parallel lines — Apply d = |C1 - C2| / sqrt(A^2 + B^2) for two parallel lines written with the same A and B.
  16. Represent a straight line algebraically and solve line problems — Combine slope, the various forms of a line's equation, and the distance formulas to represent any straight line algebraically and solve problems about lines.
