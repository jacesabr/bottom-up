Integrals

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 11 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Define an antiderivative (primitive) — Recognise an antiderivative of f as a function whose derivative is f.
  2. Recognise integration as the inverse of differentiation — Understand integration (antidifferentiation) as the reverse of differentiation.
  3. Interpret the indefinite integral symbol — Read the integral sign of f dx as the whole family of antiderivatives F(x) + C.
  4. Use that equal derivatives give the same integral family — Two indefinite integrals with the same derivative differ only by a constant.
  5. Integrate by substitution (change of variable) — Substitute a new variable whose derivative occurs in the integrand to simplify the integral.
  6. Classify a rational function as proper or improper — Call a rational function proper if degree of numerator < degree of denominator, otherwise improper.
  7. Integrate by partial fraction decomposition — Split a proper rational function into simpler fractions, then integrate each piece.
  8. Integrate a product by parts — Use the integration-by-parts formula: integral of first x (integral of second) minus integral of (derivative of first x integral of second).
  9. Define the definite integral — Recognise the definite integral as a unique number, with lower limit a and upper limit b.
  10. Use the first Fundamental Theorem (A'(x) = f(x)) — Apply Theorem 1: the derivative of the area function equals the integrand.
  11. Evaluate a definite integral via the Fundamental Theorem (F(b) - F(a)) — Apply Theorem 2: the definite integral equals F(b) - F(a) for any antiderivative F of f.
