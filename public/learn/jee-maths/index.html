  1. Relations and Functions 14 concepts
    Treat a relation R from A to B as an arbitrary subset of the Cartesian product A x B, dropping the everyday idea of a 'recognisable link'.
  2. Inverse Trigonometric Functions 13 concepts
    Recall from Chapter 1 that f has an inverse only if it is one-one and onto; trig functions are not, over their natural domains.
  3. Matrices 16 concepts
    Recognise a matrix as an ordered rectangular array of numbers or functions, whose entries are its elements.
  4. Determinants 13 concepts
    Recognise the determinant as a unique number associated with each square matrix, written |A| or det A.
  5. Continuity and Differentiability 16 concepts
    Decide whether f is continuous at c by checking the limit at c equals f(c).
  6. Application of Derivatives 12 concepts
    Read dy/dx as the rate of change of y with respect to x, and chain rates through a common variable.
  7. Integrals 11 concepts
    Recognise an antiderivative of f as a function whose derivative is f.
  8. Application of Integrals 8 concepts
    Treat the area under a curve as built from thin vertical strips of area dA = y dx.
  9. Differential Equations 11 concepts
    Recognise a differential equation as one involving derivatives of a dependent variable.
  10. Vector Algebra 13 concepts
    Recognise a vector as a quantity having both magnitude and direction.
  11. Three Dimensional Geometry 10 concepts

  12. Linear Programming 15 concepts
    Identify an optimisation problem as one that seeks to maximise or minimise a quantity such as profit or cost.
  13. Probability 9 concepts
    Find P(E|F), the probability of E given that F has occurred, as P(E and F) / P(F).

    Test a collection against the well-defined criterion: a set is a collection for which membership of any object can be decided unambiguously.
