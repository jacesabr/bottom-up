Probability

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 9 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Compute a conditional probability — Find P(E|F), the probability of E given that F has occurred, as P(E and F) / P(F).
  2. Use the complement property of conditional probability — Apply P(E'|F) = 1 - P(E|F).
  3. Apply the multiplication rule of probability — Use P(E and F) = P(E) P(F|E) = P(F) P(E|F) to find the probability of a simultaneous occurrence.
  4. Test two events for independence — Recognise E and F as independent when P(E and F) = P(E) P(F).
  5. Test three events for mutual independence — Check that all pairwise products and the triple product equal the corresponding intersection probabilities.
  6. Recognise a partition of the sample space — Identify events E1..En as a partition: pairwise disjoint, exhaustive, with non-zero probability.
  7. Apply the theorem of total probability — Compute P(A) as the sum of P(Ej) P(A|Ej) over a partition E1..En.
  8. Define a random variable — Recognise a random variable as a real valued function on the sample space of a random experiment.
  9. Apply Bayes' theorem to find a reverse probability — Use Bayes' theorem to find P(Ei|A) from the priors P(Ei) and the likelihoods P(A|Ei).
