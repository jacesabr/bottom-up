Three Dimensional Geometry

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 10 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Find the direction cosines of a line — Recognise direction cosines as the cosines of the angles a directed line makes with the axes.
  2. Find the direction ratios of a line — Recognise direction ratios as any three numbers proportional to the direction cosines.
  3. Find direction cosines of a line through two points — Get direction ratios from coordinate differences, then normalise to direction cosines.
  4. Write the vector equation of a line — Express a line through point a parallel to b as r = a + lambda b.
  5. Write the Cartesian equation of a line — Express the line through (x1,y1,z1) with direction ratios a,b,c as (x-x1)/a = (y-y1)/b = (z-z1)/c.
  6. Find the angle between two lines — Get the angle from the direction vectors using cos(theta) = (b1.b2)/(|b1||b2|).
  7. Test two lines for perpendicularity or parallelism — Lines are perpendicular if a1a2 + b1b2 + c1c2 = 0, parallel if the direction ratios are proportional.
  8. Recognise skew lines — Identify skew lines as non-coplanar lines that are neither intersecting nor parallel.
  9. Define the shortest distance between two lines — Recognise the shortest distance as the smallest length of a segment joining a point on each line.
  10. Compute the shortest distance between two skew lines — Use the formula |(a2 - a1).(b1 x b2)| / |b1 x b2| for the shortest distance between skew lines.
