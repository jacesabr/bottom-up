Differential Equations

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 11 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Define a differential equation — Recognise a differential equation as one involving derivatives of a dependent variable.
  2. Find the order of a differential equation — Define the order as the order of the highest-order derivative present.
  3. Find the degree of a differential equation — Define the degree (when it exists) as the highest power of the highest-order derivative, in polynomial form.
  4. Recognise the general solution — Identify the general solution (primitive) as the solution containing arbitrary constants.
  5. Find a particular solution from conditions — Obtain a particular solution by fixing the arbitrary constants using given conditions.
  6. Solve a variables-separable equation — When dy/dx = g(x)h(y), separate the variables and integrate both sides.
  7. Recognise a homogeneous function — Identify a homogeneous function of degree n by F(kx, ky) = k^n F(x, y).
  8. Solve a homogeneous differential equation — Recognise dy/dx = F(x,y) homogeneous when F is degree zero; solve by the substitution y = vx.
  9. Recognise a first order linear differential equation — Identify the standard form dy/dx + Py = Q with P, Q functions of x only.
  10. Compute the integrating factor — Form the integrating factor I.F. = e^(integral of P dx) for a linear equation.
  11. Solve a first order linear differential equation — Apply the standard steps: write standard form, find the I.F., then y(I.F.) = integral of (Q x I.F.) dx + C.
