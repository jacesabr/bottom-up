Sequences and Series

This chapter of JEE Maths covers 13 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Apply the laws of exponents (and solve by equating bases) — Combine and simplify powers of the same base with the product, power and quotient rules, then turn an equation like 4^(n-1) = 4^8 into the plain equation n-1 = 8.
  2. Define a sequence as an ordered list given by a rule — Recognise a sequence as an ordered arrangement of numbers (its terms) generated by a rule, and view it as a function on the natural numbers.
  3. Classify a sequence as finite or infinite — Decide whether a sequence is finite (a fixed number of terms) or infinite (it never ends).
  4. Find the nth (general) term of a sequence — Read off or substitute into the formula for a_n to obtain any required term of a sequence.
  5. Form the series of a sequence and write it in sigma notation — Build the series a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + ... from a sequence and write it compactly with the summation symbol.
  6. Recognise a geometric progression by a constant ratio — Test whether a sequence is a GP by checking that each term (after the first) bears a constant non-zero ratio to the one before it.
  7. Identify the first term a and common ratio r of a GP — Pick out a (the first term) and r (the common ratio) so a GP can be written as a, ar, ar^2, ar^3, ....
  8. Find the nth term of a GP using a_n = ar^(n-1) — Apply a_n = ar^(n-1) to compute a stated term of a GP, or to find which term equals a given value.
  9. Sum the first n terms of a GP — Use S_n = a(r^n - 1)/(r - 1) for r != 1 (and S_n = na for r = 1) to add the first n terms of a GP.
  10. Compute the geometric mean of two positive numbers — Find the geometric mean of two positive numbers a and b as sqrt(ab), the middle term that makes a, G, b a GP.
  11. Insert n geometric means between two numbers — Insert several numbers between a and b so the whole list is a GP, by treating b as the last term and solving for r.
  12. Relate the AM and GM of two positive numbers (A >= G) — Use A = (a+b)/2 and G = sqrt(ab) with the inequality A >= G to find or compare two positive numbers.
  13. Solve sequence and GP problems using terms, sums and means — Combine the nth-term and sum formulas with the geometric mean and the AM-GM relation to solve any GP-based sequence-and-series problem.
