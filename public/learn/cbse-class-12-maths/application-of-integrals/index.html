Application of Integrals

This chapter of CBSE Class 12 Mathematics covers 8 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Form the elementary area of a thin strip — Treat the area under a curve as built from thin vertical strips of area dA = y dx.
  2. Find the area under a curve with respect to the x-axis — Compute area bounded by y = f(x), the x-axis and x = a, x = b as the integral of y dx from a to b.
  3. Find the area with respect to the y-axis — Compute area bounded by x = g(y), the y-axis and y = c, y = d as the integral of x dy from c to d.
  4. Take the numerical value of area below the x-axis — When the curve dips below the x-axis the integral is negative; take its numerical (absolute) value as the area.
  5. Combine areas above and below the x-axis — When a curve is partly above and partly below the axis, add the absolute pieces: A = |A1| + A2.
  6. Find the area enclosed by a circle using integration — Use symmetry and integration of sqrt(a^2 - x^2) to recover the circle's area pi a^2.
  7. Find the area enclosed by an ellipse using integration — Use symmetry and integration to get the area pi a b enclosed by an ellipse.
  8. Find the area of a region bounded by curves and lines — Set up and evaluate the definite integral(s) for the area of a region bounded by given curves, axes and lines.
