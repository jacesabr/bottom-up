Probability

This chapter of CBSE Class 12 Mathematics covers 9 concepts, taught bottom-up. The AI tutor builds each from the ground up and checks you understand it before the next. Here's what you'll work through:

  1. Compute a conditional probability — Shrink the sample space to F, then measure how much of it is covered by E.
  2. Use the complement property of conditional probability — Once you know P(E|F), get P(E'|F) in one step by subtracting from 1.
  3. Apply the multiplication rule of probability — Rearrange the conditional probability formula to find P(E intersect F) from a product of two probabilities.
  4. Test two events for independence — Two events are independent when knowing one occurred does not change the other's probability.
  5. Test three events for mutual independence — Three events need four separate product conditions — three pairwise and one triple — to be mutually independent.
  6. Recognise a partition of the sample space — A partition slices S into non-overlapping, exhaustive pieces each with positive probability.
  7. Apply the theorem of total probability — Break P(A) into weighted conditional probabilities over every piece of a partition.
  8. Define a random variable — A random variable assigns a single real number to each outcome of a random experiment.
  9. Apply Bayes' theorem to find a reverse probability — Bayes' theorem inverts a conditional probability: from likelihoods P(A|Ei) to causes P(Ei|A).
