what we do

one concept at a time, until you've actually got it.

  • open the app and you see the topics — exactly what we promise you'll master.
  • tap a chapter and the AI tutor opens, owning one small slice — think one paper out of a 100-paper textbook.
  • you learn by talking: it explains a concept, works out whether you only think you understand, and keeps going until it's sure you do.
  • pass a short exam and move on. you can't skip ahead — each topic opens only once you've earned the one before it.
why it's special

no human can do this. now AI can.

  • this kind of 1:1 tutoring is unaffordable — even for a billionaire's kid.
  • no human can hold every grain of a subject in their head, teach it on the fly, follow every thread, and miss nothing — let alone for 10 minutes whenever you feel like it.
  • the unlock: AI can now see. it reads your handwritten working, finds exactly where you went wrong, builds the fix, and teaches it back — impossible until now.
  • so the hard sciences — maths, physics, chemistry, engineering — become as easy to learn as English, for anyone who wants it. and it only gets cheaper and better.
the vision

active, 1:1 learning — for anything worth learning.

  • academic: you sit down, a screen fills your view, and an AI that knows exactly where you left off teaches you out loud — drawing it live, making you teach it back, catching what you're weak on.
  • practical: a plumber-in-training fixes a pipe leak in a robot simulation, the AI guiding every step — and the same system trains the people who'll run the robots.

that's the destination — not today's product. today it's a live maths tutor for CBSE & JEE.

part two
questions you're going to ask
answered straight. i'd rather name the gaps than have you find them.
isn't this just a ChatGPT wrapper / another tutoring app?
no — the product isn't the chat, it's the whole syllabus mapped so the app finds your exact gap and won't let you skip it. ChatGPT will teach the hard thing and happily let you fail the easy thing underneath; that's the part a wrapper can't fake.
what does a student actually cost you? (unit economics)
~₹90 of compute per student/month even for a heavy daily user (DeepSeek API + NVIDIA's vision API) against ₹1,000 revenue — a ~91% margin, flat at any scale. the free NVIDIA tier covers the first ~100 students at ₹0.
how do you acquire students — what's CAC?
near-zero to start — a free product that spreads by word of mouth in the JEE/CBSE prep communities students already live in (Reddit, Telegram, school groups). we're building toward a CAC well under one month's fee, so payback is near-instant.
why would they stay — retention?
retention is built into the loop — you always have exactly one next concept open (never a blank wall), and every pass earns a 10-minute game as a reward and a break. exam prep is a months-long grind with a hard deadline, so a tutor that keeps finding and fixing your gaps is something you come back to daily.
why won't Byju's / Khan / Google just do this?
the real alternatives — coaching centres (₹1.5–5L), free YouTube, Byju's / Physics Wallah / Khan — either cost a fortune or just hand you more video. what's hard to copy is the whole syllabus mapped + strict gating at a price they aren't built to hit; a head start, not a forever moat.
if it works, what does the world look like in 3–5 years?
an Indian kid preps for any maths exam with a ₹1,000/month tutor that finds the gap, not a ₹2-lakh coaching seat. then physics, chemistry, SAT — one method, many subjects.
what could kill this?
two real ones: the gating could feel like a cage and students bounce (unproven till real use), and distribution — building it was easy, getting it into students' hands isn't. the raise targets exactly those.
who's building it, and what exactly does the raise buy?
Suhas & Jace — two founders who've already shipped two complete products in unrelated fields (see the team section). the money buys a lean team to get from built to verified with real students; exact amount + runway, covered live.
