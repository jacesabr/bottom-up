02 Why it stays broken

The thing that works has never been affordable.

One-to-one, mastery-based tutoring reliably beats the classroom — the best human tutors by roughly 0.8 standard deviations (Bloom's honest figure, not the inflated "2-sigma" the EdTech world likes to quote). Bloom called it a problem for one reason: no society can afford a tutor for every child.

India's answer is the coaching factory — and it is buckling:

03 The insight

Don't teach topics. Start from the question and descend to the gap.

Sit the student in front of a real past-paper question. The moment they break, walk them down to the exact prerequisite they're missing — then bring them back.

That is the diagnosis the system never performs. It's Feynman's principle turned into control flow: what you cannot reconstruct, you do not understand — find the bedrock idea, rebuild from there.

04 The product — live today

The loop.

  • Pose a real board questionThe exam paper is the spine — actual past-paper questions, in order.
  • Detect the struggleWhen the student stalls, the engine reads the gap — not their mood.
  • Descend the concept graphA "director" routes down a prerequisite graph to the precise missing concept, anywhere in the subject.
  • Teach it, then prove itConversational teaching, then a deterministic gate — MCQ/numeric/symbolic graded by code; answer keys never leave the server.
  • Teleport backOn mastery, jump straight back to the original question and re-ask it. Pass → advance to the next.

Not a ChatGPT wrapper. The LLM is swappable behind a router; the "brain" is the authored concept graph plus the orchestration. Strip the model out and the pedagogy still stands.

07 Business model

Metered today by necessity. Funding is what makes it free.

Right now the product is metered — 50 free questions, then ₹1,000/month — and it's live in production: Dodo Payments in live_mode, webhook-reconciled. (Verified against the live Render config.) Pre-funding, we can't subsidise free access, so the meter stays on to keep it sustainable.

The raise changes that. With runway plus incorporation unlocking free compute credits (see the ask), we make the product free and put it in students' hands — and focus entirely on improving it. Monetisation is already proven-buildable (it's live today), so when growth justifies our own infrastructure, we turn it back on at scale. The pricing follows the reason: sustainable now → free to grow → revenue at scale.
