part one
the pitch
problem → why now → the method → the team → the market → the ask.
the problem

students fail on something simpler, a few chapters back.

  • they don't fail the hard thing. they fail the easy thing underneath it — a prerequisite three chapters back nobody found.
  • tutoring fixes this — a good tutor is the highest-leverage help we know of — but it's unaffordable: coaching runs ₹1.5–5 lakh a year.
  • apps just throw more videos at you. more content isn't the gap; the missing prerequisite is.
why now

1:1 socratic tutoring stopped needing a human by the hour.

  • frontier AI is now good enough — and cheap enough — to teach a concept and check the student actually got it, at near-zero cost.
  • that's the unlock. it makes ₹1,000/month 1:1 coaching possible at all.
  • two years ago the cost floor made this impossible.
the method

don't skip ahead. build it up, gate every step.

  1. start at the foundation — the first concept in the chapter, nothing assumed.
  2. the ai teaches it — short socratic turns, ticking off each idea as you show it.
  3. a gate checks it — pass and the next concept opens; miss and it re-teaches. no failing out.
  4. climb the chapter, then the course — strictly in order, so you never meet an idea before its prerequisites.
  5. earn one clean exam — a real past paper, unlocked only after every concept is passed.

strict order, nothing assumed. the method — find the gap, rebuild from it — is the product, not the chatbot.

unit economics

~₹90 of compute per student. they pay ₹1,000.

  • a heavy, near-daily student: ~4 sessions a week × ~100 turns ≈ 1,700 AI messages/month, about 20% handwriting reads.
  • that's ~6M tokens/month. on the DeepSeek API for teaching + NVIDIA's API for the handwriting reads, that's ~₹90/student/month.

inference cost / student / month~₹90
gross margin on compute~91%

and that's the heavy case — flat at any scale (a per-message API, no GPUs). the free NVIDIA tier covers the first ~100 students at ₹0.

part two
questions you're going to ask
answered straight. i'd rather name the gaps than have you find them.
isn't this just a ChatGPT wrapper / another tutoring app?
no. the product isn't the chat — it's the whole syllabus mapped so the app finds your exact gap and won't let you skip it. ChatGPT will teach the hard thing and happily let you fail the easy thing under it. that's the part a wrapper can't fake.
what does a student actually cost you? (unit economics)
see unit economics above — ~₹90 of compute per student/month even for a heavy daily user (DeepSeek API for teaching, NVIDIA's API for the handwriting reads) against ₹1,000 revenue = ~91% margin, flat at any scale. free NVIDIA tier covers the first ~100 students at ₹0. no CLV:CAC yet — that needs a real CAC, post-launch.
how do you acquire students — what's CAC?
none yet — pre-launch. plan: free product → word of mouth in exam-prep communities → measure. i won't quote a CAC i haven't paid.
why would they stay — retention?
none yet either. the bet's built into the loop: 1) you always have exactly one next concept open — never a blank wall; 2) each pass earns a 10-minute game — a reward and a break when focus fades. to prove with real users. milestone #1 of the raise.
why won't Byju's / Khan / Google just do this?
they could. the real alternatives today are coaching centres (₹1.5–5L), free YouTube, and Byju's / Physics Wallah / Khan. what's hard to copy: the whole syllabus mapped + strict gating — a generic LLM tutor won't refuse to teach the next thing until the basics are passed — plus a price they aren't built to hit. a head start, not a forever moat. i won't pretend it's one.
if it works, what does the world look like in 3–5 years?
an Indian kid preps for a maths exam with a ₹1,000/month tutor that finds the gap — not a ₹2-lakh coaching seat. then physics, chemistry, SAT. one method, many subjects.
what could kill this?
three real ones: 1) the gating feels like a cage and students bounce — unproven till real use. 2) inference prices spike — but it's ~₹90/student even for a heavy user, on a competitive API market, so there's headroom. 3) distribution — building it was easy; getting it to students isn't solved. the raise targets #1 and #3.
who's building it, and what exactly does the raise buy?
who's building it: see the team section above — Suhas & Jace, who've shipped two complete products in unrelated fields. exact ask amount + runway are covered live. what the money does is fixed: a lean team to get sarthi from built to verified with real students.
